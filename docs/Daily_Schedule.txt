Definitions
-----------
- Primitive: short LQR-stabilised manoeuvre with entry checks, finite horizon, exit checks, metrics, and failure labels.
- LQR controller: time-invariant local feedback controller embedded inside one primitive, identified by `controller_id`, `linearisation_id`, Q/R weights, gain checksum, and closed-loop audit status.
- Primitive-controller variant: the active library item combining one primitive entry set, reference state/command, finite horizon, local LQR Q/R/K, exit checks, failure labels, and claim status.
- Environment context: local flow and safety features computed from the current state and wind field.
- Episode: one simulated or physical launch from the fixed nominal launch gate.
- W0/W1 dense generation: the heavy primitive-library creation stage. W0 uses no updraft. W1 uses the simple variable-Gaussian plume surrogate for both single-fan and four-fan cases. Each primitive-controller variant receives its own LQR tuning. All solved, blocked, weak, failed, rejected, continuation-valid, and x-y terminal-useful rows are preserved.
- W2 dense survival sweep: fixed-LQR replay using the single-fan and four-fan GP-corrected annular-Gaussian surrogate (`annular-GP` in the repository), panel-wise wind, feedback latency, command timing, actuator lag, and realistic termination. No hidden LQR retuning.
- W3 dense survival sweep: fixed-LQR replay under domain randomisation of updraft parameters, fan positions, fan number, glider model parameters, feedback latency, command timing, and actuator delay. No hidden LQR retuning.
- Post-W3 library-size cross-study: the heavy, balanced, light, and no-clustering/no-merging comparison of W3-surviving primitive-controller variants before any validation library-size condition is accepted.
- Full-loop validation: frozen post-W3 library-size condition + outcome model + viability governor + selector + directional 3D residual memory, tested across W0--W3 and then in real repeated launches.
- Real flight: fixed-gate repeated-launch validation with Vicon-measured initial states.
- Deadline: 15 June 2026.
- Writing protection: 08--15 June is protected for thesis writing and final integration.

Global execution rules
----------------------
- The active primitive inner loop is time-invariant LQR only.
- The LQR stabiliser is part of each primitive-controller variant, not a free-standing controller bank.
- Do not implement TVLQR in the active workflow.
- Do not preserve the previous PD-like bounded controller as a fallback, baseline, or ablation.
- Do not use LQR-tree or funnel-library terminology to brand the method.
- If LQR synthesis fails for a primitive, label the controller or rollout as blocked; do not substitute PD.
- Do not resurrect fan-layout-specific controller logic.
- Do not make reachable-state chaining a required success gate.
- Single-fan, four-fan, shifted-fan, and fan-power cases are environment instances, not separate algorithms.
- W labels are fidelity/evidence layers only, not online algorithm branches.
- Strict surrogate ladder: W0 is dry air only, W1 is variable Gaussian plume only, W2 is GP-corrected annular-Gaussian only, and W3 is randomised GP-corrected annular-Gaussian only.
- W0/W1 generate and tune a rich primitive-controller library; they do not output a 12--24 retained shortlist.
- W2/W3 replay fixed primitive-controller variants and shrink only by survival evidence; they do not regenerate, retune, or mutate LQR gains, Q/R weights, horizons, references, or entry sets.
- Clustering and merging happen only after W3.
- X/y boundary exits are retained as terminal episode evidence when appropriate; they are not continuation-valid states and must not be deleted merely because a launch reaches the lateral boundary.
- Dense runs must use chunked, resumable, compressed, worker-enabled execution.
- Dense-run default: workers=8, max_workers=8, storage_format=auto/parquet/csv_gz, compression_level=1, resume=true.
- No generated file may exceed 100 MB; preferred limit is 75 MB.
- Real-flight transfer claims require matched real logs and sim-real replay.
