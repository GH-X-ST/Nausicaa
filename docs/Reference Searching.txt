## Purpose

Use this guide when searching, selecting, and using references for the Nausicaa glider thesis and paper-style writing. The target contribution is robotics-style: real system, measured uncertain flow, environment-conditioned primitive selection, repeated-launch lift learning, viability filtering, and sim-to-real transfer.

The goal is not to collect many references. The goal is to choose credible references that directly support the thesis claims, methods, and contrasts.

## Reference quality hierarchy

Use references in this order unless there is a clear reason not to:

1. Peer-reviewed papers in top robotics venues: IJRR, IEEE T-RO, Science Robotics, RA-L, RSS, ICRA, IROS.
2. Peer-reviewed aerospace/control venues when directly relevant: Journal of Guidance, Control, and Dynamics, Journal of Aircraft, AIAA Journal, Aerospace Science and Technology, IEEE Control Systems Magazine, Automatica, IEEE TAC.
3. High-quality review papers.
4. Well-known foundational papers or books.
5. Preprints, theses, technical reports, and documentation only when necessary.

## Search scope

Search these categories:

1. fixed-wing autonomous soaring and local lift exploitation;
2. wind-field-aware UAV control;
3. gust-aware flight and low-altitude wind interaction;
4. motion primitives and primitive libraries;
5. viability kernels, reference governors, reachability, and safety filters;
6. environment-conditioned policies and contextual decision making;
7. domain randomisation and sim-to-real transfer, especially training/validation separation and explicit adaptation loops;
8. repeated physical rollouts and episodic learning;
9. post-W3 trajectory or rollout clustering and merging for representative selection;
10. primitive-local feedback controllers embedded in motion primitives;
11. real aerial-robot experiments under environmental uncertainty.

## Project-specific reference roles

Every reference should have one role:

```text
foundation
robotics precedent
method support
environment support
contrast
survey
implementation
```

Do not cite a paper only because it contains a similar keyword.

## Minimum reference standard by claim

Major method claims need at least one peer-reviewed method reference or one well-known foundational reference.

Real-world application motivation needs at least one credible robotics, aerospace, or environmental source. Avoid broad unsupported statements about urban wind.

Hardware/platform statements should use primary documentation or direct measurement.
