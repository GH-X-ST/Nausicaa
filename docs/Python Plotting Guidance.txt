# Python Plotting Guidance — Robotics-Journal Figures

For current repair-cycle plots, include 0.10 s primitive horizon compliance, 5 controller-input-slot compliance at a 20 ms update period, directional 3D residual belief behaviour, safe exploration/exploitation traces, heavy/balanced/light/no-clustering library-size cases, 0, 5, 10, 20, 50, and 100 launch history curves, and mark rejected v4.10-style evidence as `diagnostic_not_passed` where shown.

## Purpose

Use this guide when creating plots for thesis, slides, logs, or robotics-style paper figures. Figures should show physical evidence, not decorative output.

## General style

Use:

- white background;
- clear axes and units;
- restrained colours;
- readable labels;
- deterministic export;
- captions that state the claim;
- high-resolution PNG for thesis figures.

Recommended export:

```python
fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(5.2, 3.0), dpi=600)
fig.patch.set_facecolor("white")
ax.set_facecolor("white")
...
fig.savefig(out_path, dpi=600, facecolor="white", bbox_inches="tight")
plt.close(fig)
```

## File-size rule

Every generated plot file should remain below 100 MB. Prefer below 25 MB for thesis figures and below 75 MB for dense diagnostics. If a plot exceeds 100 MB, simplify it, rasterise dense layers, split it into panels, or move full data to compressed partitions.

## Figure types needed

### 1. Environment context map

Show:

- arena outline;
- local updraft field;
- context features such as wing-mean wind or spanwise gradient;
- fixed launch gate or representative current state;
- safety volume.

### 2. Primitive outcome map

Show accepted, weak, failed, and rejected primitive outcomes over physically meaningful axes:

```text
speed
heading error
wing-mean wind
left-right wind difference
wall margin
energy residual
lift dwell time
```

Also show `episode_terminal_useful` or `boundary_use_class == "episode_terminal_useful"` separately when x/y wall or lateral safety-volume exits are retained as terminal episode evidence. Do not colour these rows as continuation success.

### 3. Repeated-launch episode traces

Show multiple episodes with:

- trajectory path;
- accepted primitive;
- termination point;
- local updraft contour;
- directional 3D residual belief before/after where useful.

Do not overlay too many episodes in one plot.

### 4. Belief evolution

Show:

- directional 3D residual belief before and after repeated launches;
- observed lift, energy, and dwell residual evidence;
- decay setting;
- history length at 0, 5, 10, 20, 50, and 100 launches;
- capture, dwell, safety, and selection-change metrics over launch index.

### 5. Sim-real pairing

Show predicted versus measured:

- energy residual;
- lift dwell time;
- capture success;
- termination cause;
- minimum wall margin.

### 6. Generalisation test

Show fidelity-layer and environment-instance effects without turning them into online branches:

- shifted fan position;
- changed fan power;
- active fan subset;
- randomised updraft amplitude or width.

Use W0 as dry air only, W1 as variable Gaussian plume only, W2 as GP-corrected annular-Gaussian only, and W3 as randomised GP-corrected annular-Gaussian only. Do not present W0/W1 clustering as the final library-compression result.

### 7. Post-W3 library compression


Show the four required library-size cases separately:

```text
heavy clustering and merging
balanced clustering and merging
light clustering and merging
no-clustering/no-merging
```

Figures must show how library size affects safety success, selected primitive diversity, lift capture, dwell, no-viable rate, and memory/exploration selection changes. Do not present one compact-library case as the final method unless the cross-study supports it.

Show clustering and merging only after W3 survival filtering. Figures should distinguish:

- W0/W1 generated variants;
- W2 eliminated or downgraded variants;
- W3 eliminated or downgraded variants;
- W3 survivors selected as medoids or merged representatives;
- continuation-valid versus episode-terminal-useful cases.

## Colour guidance

Use consistent semantic colours:

```text
accepted / safe: green
weak / marginal: amber
failed / unsafe: red
rejected before execution: grey
baseline: blue or dark grey
context-conditioned controller: green or purple
```

Do not use rainbow maps for categorical outcomes.

## Label rules

Every axis must include units where applicable:

```text
x_w (m)
y_w (m)
z_w (m)
speed (m s^{-1})
yaw error (deg)
energy residual (m)
lift dwell time (s)
minimum wall margin (m)
wing-mean vertical wind (m s^{-1})
```

## Caption standard

Good caption:

```text
Primitive outcome map under measured-updraft replay. The accepted region expands with wing-mean vertical wind, while low-speed and low-margin cases remain rejected by the viability governor.
```

Poor caption:

```text
Results of simulation and real flight.
```
